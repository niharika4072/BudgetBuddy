📈 Investment Scheme Calculator 6 Schemes

Compare how your money grows across popular Indian investment options.

Monthly Investment Amount (₹)
What is this? The fixed amount you invest every single month — like a subscription to your own future. It goes in automatically, whether the market is up or down.
📌 Example: You invest ₹500 every month. After 12 months you've put in ₹6,000 without even thinking about it. This is called a SIP (Systematic Investment Plan).
₹
Investment Duration (Years)
What is this? How many years you plan to keep your money invested without withdrawing it. The longer you stay invested, the more your money grows.
📌 Example: Investing for 10 years instead of 5 can more than double your returns — because your interest also earns interest over time!
Lump-sum Investment (optional)
What is this? A single, one-time payment you make all at once — instead of paying small amounts monthly. You put the whole amount in on day one and let it grow.
📌 Example: You got ₹10,000 as a birthday gift. Instead of spending it, you put the whole amount into an FD on day one. That is a lump-sum investment.
₹
Expected Annual Return (for Custom %)
What is this? How much your money grows in one full year, shown as a percentage. If the annual return is 10%, your ₹100 becomes ₹110 by end of the year.
📌 Example: ₹1,000 at 12% annual return → ₹1,120 after year 1 → ₹1,254 after year 2. The growth builds on top of previous growth — this is called compound interest!

Scheme Invested Returns Maturity Value
What is Maturity Value? The total money you receive at the END of your investment period. It includes everything you put in PLUS all the profit/interest you earned on top.
📌 Example: You invested ₹18,000 over 3 years. At 12% return, maturity value = ₹21,500. The extra ₹3,500 is pure profit from the market.

* Returns are approximate. SIP/Mutual Fund returns depend on market performance. PPF, NSC, SSY, and FD rates are indicative based on current government rates. Always consult a financial advisor before investing.
